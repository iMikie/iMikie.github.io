The cheapest developer you can hire is yourself.

 

 

As my career has progressed I've moved more and more into management, venture raising, even sales, but more away from day-to-day engineering.  Yet I often still find myself asking, "is such-and-such possible?" 

 

This is where being your own developer comes in.  Good developers are expensive and busy.  And no matter how good they are, do you really want to take someone else's word that something is impossible? 

 

This year I'm getting a tune-up on my old UC Berkeley CS degree.  I've enrolled in Dev BootCamp, one of the growing number of insanely intensive trade schools that teach Web or Mobile technologies in 9 or 18 weeks. 

 

• What's it like to learn with people half your age?

• How have things changed (or not) at the ground level?

• Can I bring a different perspective to it all?

• How does this generation view the startup culture?

 

That's what I'll explore here.  Now, some of these posts are required by Dev BootCamp but I'll be adding my own reflections...and a bunch of free code.
